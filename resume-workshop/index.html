How to build & customize your resume with AI in under 10 minutes.

A step-by-step guide to creating a tailored, job-ready resume using Claude. No templates. No fluff. Just a system that works.

By Giovannie Zounon · A Giovannie Labs production

Start the guide Build the system Open Claude

Most people send the same resume to every job and wonder why they never hear back. The problem isn't your experience. It's that your resume doesn't speak the language of the job description. This guide walks you through building a base resume with Claude, then setting up a system that tailors it to any role in minutes. You'll do this once and use it forever.

Phase 1

Build your base resume.

The master copy that everything else is built from. Spend real time here. Sharpness compounds.

01
1

Gather your raw materials

Before you open Claude, spend 10 minutes pulling together:

  • Your current resume or LinkedIn profile, even if it's outdated
  • 2 to 3 job descriptions for roles you'd actually want
  • A list of your top accomplishments with numbers attached: users reached, revenue generated, projects shipped, people trained

Why this matters: Claude is powerful, but it can't invent your career. The better your raw material, the sharper your resume. Think of it like cooking. Claude is the chef. You bring the ingredients.

02
2

Start a conversation with Claude

Go to claude.ai and start a new chat. Don't just say "write me a resume." Give Claude context to work with.

Example prompt

I want to create a one-page resume targeting [type of role, e.g., "AI/Tech corporate roles"]. Here's my background: - Current role: [Your title] at [Company] - Previous role: [Title] at [Company] where I [top 2-3 accomplishments] - Skills: [List your technical and strategic skills] - Education: [Degree, school] - Languages: [If applicable] - Side projects or leadership: [Anything notable] Create a clean, one-page resume with these sections: Professional Summary, Professional Experience (with bullet points starting with action verbs), Technical Skills, Leadership & Impact, and Education. Quantify impact wherever possible.
Pro tip. If you have your resume as a file, upload it directly and say "Use this as the base and improve it for [target role type]."
03
3

Iterate until it's sharp

Your first draft won't be perfect. That's fine. Push Claude to make it better:

  • "Make the summary more specific to AI governance roles."
  • "The IBM bullets are too generic. Add numbers and outcomes."
  • "Reorder the skills section to lead with what's most relevant to enterprise AI."
  • "This is two pages. Cut it to one without losing impact."

The goal: a resume that passes the "so what?" test. Every single bullet should answer the question, "so what did that accomplish?"

04
4

Export as a Word document

Once you're happy with the content, ask Claude to generate a .docx file:

Create this resume as a Word document. Clean, minimal design. One page. Use a professional layout with clear section headers and consistent formatting.

Claude will generate a downloadable .docx you can save and edit. This becomes your base resume: the master copy that everything else is built from.

Phase 2

Set up your resume tailoring system.

This is where it stops being a one-off chat and becomes your personal resume strategist on call.

05
5

Create a Claude Project

A Claude Project lets you save instructions and files that persist across every new conversation. Think of it as building your own custom AI assistant.

How to do it

  1. Go to claude.ai on desktop. Projects aren't available on mobile.
  2. Click Projects in the left sidebar.
  3. Click Create Project.
  4. Name it something like "Resume Tailor".
06
6

Upload your base resume

Inside your new project:

  1. Click Add Knowledge or the file upload area.
  2. Upload the .docx resume you created in Step 4.
  3. This file is now available to every conversation in this project.
07
7

Add project instructions

This is the brain of your system. Click on Project Instructions and paste in a prompt that tells Claude exactly how to behave. Here's a template you can customize:

## Role You are my personal resume strategist. You take job descriptions and produce a tailored, one-page resume based on my uploaded base resume. ## When I Paste a Job Description 1. Analyze it for key requirements, must-have skills, and recurring language 2. Map my experience to the job's priorities 3. Rewrite my resume with targeted adjustments: - Reorder bullets so the most relevant experience comes first - Mirror the job description's terminology naturally - Adjust the Professional Summary to speak to what this employer cares about - Keep it to ONE page 4. After the resume, give me a Fit Analysis: - Top 3 strengths for this role - Any gaps and how to address them in a cover letter - Keywords from the JD that are now in the resume ## Rules - Never fabricate experience or skills I don't have - Never exceed one page - Start every bullet with a strong action verb - If the JD asks for something I haven't done, flag it honestly - No buzzword fluff ("results-driven professional," "passionate team player")
08
8

Test it with a real job description

Open a new chat inside your project. Paste a job description you're interested in. Watch it work.

If the output isn't exactly right, refine your project instructions. The first version is never the final version. Common tweaks:

  • "Always emphasize my bilingual capabilities when the role mentions international."
  • "Lead with my governance experience for compliance-heavy roles."
  • "If the role is startup-focused, highlight my consulting practice first."
Phase 3

Use it every time you apply.

From now on, applying to a job is a 5-minute task, not an afternoon.

09
9

The 5-minute application workflow

Your process for every job application looks like this:

  1. Find a role you want. 2 min reading the JD.
  2. Open your Resume Tailor project in Claude.
  3. Start a new chat, paste the JD. 10 seconds.
  4. Review the tailored resume and fit analysis. 2 min.
  5. Copy the content into your Word template, or ask Claude to generate a new .docx. 1 min.

You just went from one generic resume to a custom-tailored application in under 5 minutes. Do that for 10 jobs and you'll see a real difference in response rates.

10
10

Keep your base resume updated

Every time you have a new win at work, a completed project, a metric you can quantify, update your base resume in the project's Knowledge Files. Your system is only as good as the raw material it's working with.

Examples of things to add

  • "Launched an AI governance framework adopted by 3 departments."
  • "Trained 150 employees on AI tools, increasing adoption by 40%."
  • "Reduced onboarding time by 2 weeks through automated learning pathways."

Stop sending the same resume to every job.

The difference between getting interviews and getting ignored usually isn't your experience. It's how well your resume communicates that experience to a specific employer. This system takes 10 minutes to build and saves you hours on every application going forward. Set it up once. Use it every time.
