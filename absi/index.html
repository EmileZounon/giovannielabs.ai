The problem

Choosing a future should not feel like guesswork.

Many students have talent and ambition, but not enough guidance, visibility, or access to the right opportunities. ABSI helps students move from confusion to clarity through structured STEM pathway support.

24%
Disease burden, 3% of the workforce

Africa carries 24% of the global disease burden, but has only about 3% of the world's health workforce.

<100
Researchers per million

Sub-Saharan Africa has fewer than 100 researchers per million people, compared with over 4,000 in developed economies.

Millions
Locked out of STEM

Millions of talented students never enter the STEM workforce, not for lack of ability, but for lack of guidance and opportunity.

The problem is not talent. It is navigation, access, and opportunity.

Why ABSI exists

The navigation system African students deserve.

ABSI combines AI guidance, pathway information, mentorship, and hands-on STEM experiences so students can make informed decisions about their future.

AI Advisor

Personalized career guidance tailored to each student's voice, context, and ambition.

Pathway Information

Clear, accurate information on Ghanaian university and TVET opportunities that actually connect to student goals.

Mentorship

Exposure to mentors, industry insight, and networks of people already doing the work students want to do.

STEM Projects

Portfolio-building experiences, innovation challenges, and practical learning that show what a student can actually do.

Who ABSI is for

Built for students. Useful for schools and families.

ABSI is designed for secondary school students who want clearer direction, better guidance, and stronger pathways into STEM.

It also supports the schools, families, and partners who want to help students make better-informed choices about their future.
