Why this exists

Most AI talk skips the people who need it most

There are rooms full of creators, entrepreneurs, and educators in French-speaking Africa who keep hearing that AI is going to change everything. Meanwhile, the tutorials assume you already know how to use a terminal. The bar is set quietly high.

KodjoLive Masterclass lowers it. The session walks a complete beginner through building and shipping a real website using Claude, from first prompt to live URL. No prior code experience assumed. No vocabulary gatekeeping.

What you learn

The whole pipeline, not just the prompts

How to talk to Claude

Prompting patterns that work for building software, not just chatting. What to say, what to leave out, when to push back.

From idea to a real file

Turning a fuzzy idea into HTML, CSS, and JavaScript that actually runs in a browser. No hand-waving.

Shipping to a URL

Getting the site off your laptop and onto the internet so real people can see it. Domain, hosting, done.

Iterating with confidence

Fixing bugs, improving design, adding features, all without breaking what already works.

Who it's for

Builders who have been told they don't belong in the AI conversation

Entrepreneurs with a business idea and no technical co-founder. Teachers who want to make a class website for their students. Creators who have a brand and no budget for a developer. French speakers tired of translating English tutorials in their head.

You do not need a degree, a Mac, or permission.
