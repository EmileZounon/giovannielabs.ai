The Python one-liner (recommended if you have a terminal)

Install once: pip install youtube-transcript-api

from youtube_transcript_api import YouTubeTranscriptApi api = YouTubeTranscriptApi() fetched = api.fetch('VIDEO_ID_HERE', languages=['en']) for s in fetched.snippets: mm, ss = int(s.start//60), int(s.start%60) print(f'[{mm:02d}:{ss:02d}] {s.text}')

Save as transcript.py, then run: python3 transcript.py > transcript.txt

Finding the video ID: in a URL like youtube.com/watch?v=wKG7QT0rjCo, the ID is everything after v=, so wKG7QT0rjCo.
Step 2 · 30 seconds

The master prompt

This prompt is the intellectual property. Save it, tune it to your brand, reuse it on every video. Paste it into Claude (claude.ai or the app), then paste your transcript right after.

You are a short-form video editor specialized in YouTube Shorts, TikTok, and Instagram Reels. Here is the transcript of a video, with timestamps: [PASTE THE TRANSCRIPT HERE] Analyze it and return the 5 best moments to cut into short clips (20 to 60 seconds each). For each moment, provide: 1. Start and end timestamp 2. Duration in seconds 3. Virality rating out of 5 stars 4. The exact quote that makes the moment powerful 5. Why it works (one sentence) 6. A hook line for the title or caption Rank them from most clippable to least clippable. Selection criteria: - Standalone insight (no context needed to understand) - Narrative tension (hook, suspense, payoff) - Emotional moment (vulnerability, surprise, humor) - Memorable quote (lines that work as on-screen text) - Pattern interrupt (reframe, counter-intuitive, unexpected) No em-dashes. Clean format with emoji ranks (🥇 🥈 🥉).
Pro tip: add one extra line tuned to your niche. For a coaching channel: "Prioritize moments that teach a single actionable lesson." For a storytelling channel: "Prioritize moments with emotional arc: setup, tension, release."

Step 3 · 1 minute

Review Claude's picks

Claude returns 5 moments, ranked, with everything you need to decide. Skim, pick your top 3, archive the rest in a "maybe later" doc.

What to look for:

  • Is the quote strong enough to carry 30 seconds of attention?
  • Does the moment make sense without the rest of the video?
  • Is the hook text punchy enough to stop a scroll?

If Claude's top pick doesn't feel right, re-run with: "These picks aren't quite right. The video is about [topic]. Prioritize moments with [X]. Return 5 new picks." Iterate until it clicks.

Step 4 · 15 minutes

Cut in your editor

You have exact timestamps. Open the raw video in your editor, jump straight to each one, trim, export. No watching top to bottom. No scrubbing.

The Regret Minimization Framework

03:02 – 03:47 · 45 sec · ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
"The framework I would use is the regret minimization framework: looking at 85-year-old me coming back to the present and saying, 'Would I be okay with failing at this business and torpedoing the one that was working, so that I could say I had a shot?'"
Why it works: Standalone insight, no context needed. Bezos-style framework with Hormozi's spin.
Hook: "Ask your 85-year-old self this before every big decision."

The real risk isn't the money, it's the focus

"You can use your own money or other people's money. That's not the risk. The risk is that you already have something that's good, and you're going to probably lose it. Just because you're going to be split."
