About the founder

Who is behind Giovannie Labs, and why the lab exists at all

Giovannie Labs is the work of Emile Giovannie Zounon, an educator and technologist based in Boston. He trained as a teacher and spent several years in the classroom designing learning experiences that helped his students move toward real economic mobility. The software studio grew out of that work, once it became clear that many of the tools his students and collaborators actually needed were either too expensive, too far removed from their daily reality, or simply written in languages they did not read fluently.

The lab now builds for specific people in specific places. Senior high school students in Ghana choosing between university pathways. A cleaning and laundry business in Benin trying to figure out where the money was going. Resident assistants at a small college buried in spreadsheets. French-speaking creators who had quietly decided that the AI tutorials everyone kept linking them to were written for someone else.

Most products start from a single conversation with one of those people. Emile keeps the studio small on purpose, so he can stay in that conversation as the product grows.
