What it does

Everything housing touches, in one place

Small colleges and boarding schools run housing on email threads, printed rosters, and brittle spreadsheets that one person maintains. When that person leaves, institutional memory goes with them.

ResLife replaces that whole stack. Students log in to see their room and request changes. RAs file incident reports from their phone. The principal opens or closes the room selection window with a single toggle. Everyone, from the dean to the new freshman, works against the same source of truth.

Core features

Built with real administrators in the loop

Student self-selection

Students browse available rooms and pick their own, or the admin keeps selection closed and assigns manually. A single toggle decides.

Room change requests

Students propose moves. Principal and staff review, approve, or deny. On approval, the system ends the old assignment and creates the new one automatically.

Bulk CSV upload

Import buildings, rooms, and student rosters from a spreadsheet in minutes. No more manual data entry on day one.

Occupancy dashboard

Live view of which rooms are filled, which beds are open, and which buildings are over or under capacity.

Incident reports

RAs log incidents from their phone. Reports are searchable and tied to students, rooms, and shifts.

Role-based access

Student, RA, staff, and admin roles. Firebase Auth with email/password and Google SSO. API routes verify tokens server-side.

For whom

Institutions that have outgrown spreadsheets but can't afford enterprise housing software

Small private colleges, boarding schools, residential programs, international academies. Schools with 100 to 2,000 students where a single administrator still owns the housing process in their head.

Already in use as a live demo at one school (Uchenna's). Ready to onboard the next.
